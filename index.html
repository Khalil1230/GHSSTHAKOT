Your Core Tool

The IDBCC Framework

One structure. Works for every topic. Every essay type. Memorise it once — use it for life. This framework is the backbone of the entire course.

I

INTRODUCTION — آغاز

The opening paragraph. Grab the judge's attention immediately. Give context. State your argument clearly. Judges decide within the first paragraph whether this essay is special.

🎣 Hook 🌍 Context 💬 Thesis
D

DEVELOPMENT 1 — پہلی دلیل

Your first supporting argument. State the point. Back it with a real example or fact. Explain why it matters to your thesis. Build your case systematically.

📌 Point 📖 Evidence 🔗 Explanation
B

BRIDGE — دوسری دلیل

Your second argument OR a counter-argument you then defeat. Use transition words: "However," "Furthermore," "On the other hand." Show the judge you see multiple angles.

🔄 Transition 📌 Point 📖 Evidence
C

CLIMAX — سب سے مضبوط دلیل

Your STRONGEST point goes here — saved for maximum impact. This is where deep thinking is rewarded. Use your most powerful example. Leave the judge impressed.

💥 Strongest Point 🇵🇰 Pakistan Example 🔗 Explanation
C

CONCLUSION — اختتام

Close with authority. Restate your thesis in new words. Summarise your two best points. End with a sentence the judge will still be thinking about after they put your essay down.

🔁 Restate Thesis 📋 Summary 🌟 Powerful Close

Phase 1

Component Mastery — 9 Skills

The core of the course. Each of the 9 essay components is taught as a standalone skill — defined, modelled, identified, then written. One skill at a time, one week at a time.

🎯 Goal: Students can write each of the 9 components in isolation with confidence and accuracy before assembling a full paragraph.
1·1
The Hook — Your Opening Weapon ہک — پہلا جملہ جو سب کو روک دے
📖 Definition & types: Quote Hook, Bold Statement Hook, Rhetorical Question Hook — explained with Pakistani examples
🔍 Spot It: Share 3 essay openings — students identify which type of hook is used (Google Form)
🚫 Forbidden Phrases lesson: "Since the dawn of time..." — why clichés lose marks immediately
✍️ Build It: Given a topic, write one hook sentence of each type — submitted via Google Form
📚 Memory Bank: 9 strong hook sentences to memorise and adapt for any topic
1·2
Context — Setting the Scene پس منظر — موضوع کو سمجھانا
📖 What context does: bridges hook to thesis, narrows the topic, shows the student knows the subject
🔍 Spot It: Identify the context sentences in 2 sample introductions
✍️ Build It: Write a 2-sentence context for a given topic using the Pakistan angle formula
👥 Peer Check: Does the context connect naturally from the hook to the thesis? Students mark each other
1·3
The Thesis — Your Essay's Spine مرکزی دلیل — مضمون کی روح
📖 What a thesis is and is NOT — clear argument vs vague statement (with comparison examples)
🔍 Spot It: Which of these 4 sentences is a strong thesis? Explain why (Google Form)
✍️ Build It: Write a thesis for 3 different topics using the template "Although X, Y because Z"
📝 Quiz: Rewrite a weak thesis into a strong one — scored and returned with feedback
1·4
Point — State Your Argument Clearly بات کہنا — واضح اور براہ راست
📖 How to open a body paragraph: topic sentence = one clear point, not a story
🔍 Spot It: Which sentence is a point and which is evidence? Sort them correctly
✍️ Build It: Write opening point sentences for 3 paragraph topics on Social Media
1·5
Evidence — Prove It With Examples ثبوت — مثال سے بات ثابت کرنا
📖 Types of evidence: Pakistan-specific examples, global examples, statistics, personal observation
🇵🇰 Pakistan Example Bank: 20 ready-to-use real-life examples covering all 7 common themes
✍️ Build It: Add evidence to 3 point sentences written in Skill 1·4 — complete the point-evidence pair
1·6
Explanation — Connect Evidence to Thesis وضاحت — مثال کو مرکزی دلیل سے جوڑنا
📖 The "So what?" principle — every piece of evidence must be explained, not just stated
🔍 Spot It: Which paragraph has explanation and which just lists facts? What is missing?
✍️ Build It: Add explanation sentences to complete the Point-Evidence-Explanation triplet
1·7
Transitions & Bridge — Linking Your Ideas ربط — خیالات کو جوڑنا
📖 10 essential transition words categorised: Adding, Contrasting, Concluding — with usage rules
🚫 Forbidden: Starting every paragraph with "Also" — why it signals weak writing
✍️ Build It: Rewrite 5 choppy sentences using correct transitions to create smooth flow
1·8
The Climax — Saving Your Best for Last عروج — سب سے اہم بات آخر میں
📖 Why the strongest argument goes third — the law of recency in judge psychology
🔍 Spot It: Rank 3 arguments from weakest to strongest — explain your reasoning
✍️ Build It: Write a climax paragraph using a specific Pakistan example that is original and vivid
1·9
The Conclusion — Close with Power اختتام — یادگار انداز میں بند کرنا
📖 3 conclusion techniques: Call to Action, Full Circle, Vision Statement — with Pakistani examples
🚫 Rule: Never copy your introduction word-for-word — rephrase, do not repeat
✍️ Build It: Write conclusions using all 3 techniques for the same topic — choose your favourite
📝 Phase 1 End Quiz: 9-question quiz — one question per skill to confirm mastery before Phase 2
Phase 2

Paragraph & Full Essay Assembly

Students now combine their 9 mastered skills into complete paragraphs, then into a full competition essay. The focus shifts from components to cohesion.

🎯 Goal: Students write a complete, structured 5-paragraph essay using IDBCC from start to finish — correctly and without prompting.
2·1
Building the Introduction Paragraph پہلا پیراگراف — Hook + Context + Thesis
🔗 Combining Skills 1+2+3 into one flowing introduction paragraph — smooth, not choppy
🔍 Annotated model: colour-coded introduction showing Hook (purple), Context (gold), Thesis (green)
✍️ Write a full introduction for an assigned topic — peer review using a 3-point checklist
2·2
Building the Body Paragraphs درمیانی پیراگراف — Point + Evidence + Explanation
🔗 Combining Skills 4+5+6 across D, B, and Climax paragraphs — using correct transitions between each
🔍 Model essay with body paragraphs annotated — identifying Point, Evidence, Explanation in each
✍️ Write all 3 body paragraphs for a given topic — arrange from weakest to strongest argument

Four Essay Types — One Framework

The IDBCC structure never changes. Only the tone, vocabulary, and focus shift. Taught in this exact order — from most structured to most personal.

Argumentative

دلیلی مضمون

Prove a position using logic, evidence, and counter-arguments. The most common type in Pakistani inter-school competitions. Taught first.

Formal tone Evidence-led Confident Taught 1st

Narrative

بیانیہ مضمون

Know the Rules

How Judges Award Marks

Understanding how marks are distributed is like seeing the exam paper before you sit it. Every lesson in this course targets one or more of these four criteria directly.

Content

30%

Relevance, depth of ideas, real-life examples, and originality of thinking. Using a specific Pakistani example scores higher than a generic global statement.

Language

30%

Vocabulary precision, grammatical accuracy, sentence variety, and rhetorical devices. Short punchy sentences mixed with longer analytical ones impress judges.

Organisation

25%

Clear structure, logical paragraph flow, and smooth transitions. The IDBCC framework directly targets and maximises this entire criterion.

Originality

15%

Fresh angle, creative opening, memorable conclusion. Avoid clichés. Judges read hundreds of essays — a specific local reference immediately stands out.
